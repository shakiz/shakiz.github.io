Delete Your Account & Data

Your worship and your data are yours. You can ask Taqwa to deactivate and delete your account at any time — from inside the app, or by email if you no longer have access.

Last updated: June 25, 2026

Delete your account in the app

The quickest way to remove your account is from within Taqwa:

  1. Open Taqwa and sign in with your Google account.
  2. Open the Profile tab from the bottom navigation.
  3. Scroll to the bottom and tap "Delete account".
  4. Read the confirmation and tap "Delete my account".

Your account is deactivated immediately and you are signed out on this device.

You have 30 days to change your mind

Deleting your account starts a 30-day grace period. During this window your account is deactivated and hidden, but not yet erased. If you simply sign back in with the same Google account within 30 days, the deletion request is automatically cancelled and your account and data are fully restored — no extra steps needed.

Request deletion by email

If you can no longer open the app, you can request deletion manually:

  1. Email shakil.play335@gmail.com from the email address linked to your Taqwa account.
  2. Use the subject line "Taqwa — Account Deletion Request".
  3. Confirm in the message that you want your account and all stored data permanently deleted.
  4. We verify the request and permanently erase your data within 30 days, then send you a confirmation email.

What gets deleted

When your account is permanently deleted, the following data is removed:

  • Profile details — your name, email address, and profile photo.
  • Worship activity — prayer and goal completions, zikr counts, daily focus history, and streaks.
  • Preferences — prayer-time adjustments, calculation method, reminder settings, and Surah bookmarks.
  • Community content — posts, comments, and reactions you created.
  • Sign-in link — the association between your Google account and Taqwa is severed.

Reference content such as the Qur'an, the 99 Names, and prayer-time calculations is not personal data and is unaffected.

Permanent and irreversible. Once the 30-day grace period passes without a return — or once an emailed deletion request is processed — your data cannot be recovered.

Questions about your data or this process? Contact us at shakil.play335@gmail.com.
